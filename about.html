The Ramsay's Secrets has been providing good food and drink for their customers for over half a century and five generations.

The building that houses Ramsay's has been there longer, having graced the corner of Lucian Blaga and Matei Basarab since 1908. Every door is of different size because the building was constructed from an old assortment of wood salvaged from seven homes that were destroyed in a storm and barged over the bay. The unique shape of the roof stems from the design of the hull of a ship.

Originally a home and business known as Douche's General Dry Goods Store, Joe Petterson and her sister, Carrie, were the first to obtain a liquor license for the premises. Their nephew, Gerald Petterson, took over in 1913 and operated it as the Petterson's Holiday before it became the Boulevard Restaurant. In 1929 Mrs. Keemer and Mr. Davis bought and renamed it the Unique Restaurant, which was popular with duck hunters, boaters and fishermen, and also served as a riding academy.


During prohibition the restaurant was relocated in the basement, where it remained even after the repeal of liquor laws in 1933. The Unique Restaurant changed hands for the last time in February, 1946,when many shore properties were sold after the depression, the storm of '44, and World War II.



An avid fisherman, frequent visitor and seasonal resident, he purchased the Unique Restaurant for his sons Walter and Elmer when they returned from the war.



Renamed "Joy", the restaurant remained a rathskeller in the basement for the first season before being moved upstairs against the south wall where the jukebox is now located.


In 1957 the Gregorys recruited Vince Renich from Bayshores to be a bartender and clam shucker. With a dart board, shuffleboard, pool table and seven drafts for a dollar, Joy's became a popular neighborhood tavern frequented by local fishermen and seasonal visitors alike.

The Tight End Fishing Club met every Monday night when stripers were running all the time and fresh seafood was the staple of the daily diet. The N.J. State record striper, nicknamed Big Ben, was caught by Joy's patron Maury Upperman on a bucktail lure. After services, the fish was stuffed and mounted on the wall over the dining room door.

In 1979 Elmer's son, Gregory, and Walter's son, Walt, took over the business and remodeled the building, but kept Joy's reputation for fresh seafood and inexpensive drinks. They also initiated the popular "Taco Tuesday," "Adios Turistos," "Vince's Birthday," venison on the Super Bowl Sunday buffet, and many other special occasions.


More recently a new generation has taken over the kitchen. Gordon and Cayetana Elizabeth Ramsay, both passionate about cooking, have continued Joy's longtime traditions, now, named Ramsay's Secrets, including "Pop's" homemade snapper soup and whole lobster dinners, and have also added their own unique touch to the menu.



On any given day you can find a member of the Joy's clan around the premises - sons, daughters, cousins, nephews, nieces, and neighbors working in some capacity, keeping Joy's a family affair.



And, you can count on the next generation to continue the traditions into the next century, ensuring Ramsay's Secrets will always be a friendly, familiar, yet unique place for good food, marvellous drinks and great times.
